MISSION & VISION

MISSION

With the ever-evolving policy and governance sphere, Applied Public Policy Strategies (APPS) empowers students to deliver innovative, data-driven solutions to complex government and policy challenges. By combining strategic consulting skills with a passion for public service, we foster impactful change and develop the next generation of civic-minded leaders at Cornell and beyond. Working with leading think tanks, non-profits, governments, and industry innovators, we strive to drive meaningful policy outcomes and create sustainable solutions that address today’s most pressing challenges.

A student-led and pre-professional organization at Cornell dedicated to exploring how emerging technologies and economic challenges are transforming governance, economics, law, and public policy. We serve as a platform for students interested in public sector consulting, legal and policy analysis, and ethical tech innovation.

FAQ

Applied Public Policy Strategies at Cornell

Welcome to Applied Public Policy Strategies at Cornell (APPS). We are a student-run organization dedicated to providing hands-on policy analysis experience through real-world projects. Below you'll find answers to common questions about our club.

FAQs (Prospective Clients)

What is Applied Public Policy Strategies at Cornell?
Applied Public Policy Strategies at Cornell (APPS) is a student-run club that provides members with practical experience in public policy analysis and consulting. We work on real policy projects, allowing students to develop their analytical skills and make meaningful contributions to important policy issues.
How are project teams structured?
Each project team consists of 5 members total, including:
  • 1 Project Manager
    • Responsibilities: leads the team, coordinates workflow, and manages client relationships.
    • Profile: upperclassman with professional experience and client management training.
  • 4 Policy Analysts
    • Responsibilities: conduct research, perform analysis, and contribute to deliverables.
    • Profile: underclassmen with significant training in common project tasks.
This structure ensures that every team member has meaningful responsibilities while maintaining effective collaboration.
What types of projects does APPS work on?
APPS takes on a variety of policy projects across different issue areas. Projects may include policy research, program evaluation, stakeholder analysis, and developing policy recommendations for real clients. The diverse project portfolio allows members to explore different areas of public policy and develop versatile analytical skills.
What makes APPS different from other policy or consulting clubs?
APPS focuses specifically on applied public policy work within government consulting, giving members hands-on experience with real policy challenges. Our project-based approach, combined with comprehensive training and small team sizes, ensures that every member gains substantial experience in policy analysis and consulting. We bridge the gap between classroom learning and professional policy work.

FAQs (Prospective Applicants)

What is the time commitment?
All APPS policy analysts are required to commit 3 to 5 hours per week. This time includes:
  • Team meetings to discuss project progress.
  • Individual research and analysis work.
  • Preparing deliverables and presentations.
  • Occasional general body meetings.
We understand that students have busy schedules, and we strive to make the commitment manageable while still providing valuable experience.
Do I need prior experience in public policy?
No prior experience is necessary. APPS provides comprehensive training to all members before they begin working on projects. Our training program covers:
  • Policy analysis frameworks and methodologies.
  • Research techniques and data analysis.
  • Professional communication and presentation skills.
  • Project management best practices.
We welcome students from all majors and backgrounds who are interested in learning about public policy.
How do I apply to join APPS?
Information about recruitment and applications is typically announced at the beginning of each semester. We encourage interested students to attend our information sessions and reach out via appliedpps@gmail.com to learn more about the club. Stay tuned to our communications for application timelines and requirements.
Can I join APPS if I'm not a Public Policy, Government, or Economics major?
Absolutely. APPS welcomes students from all majors across Cornell University. We value diverse perspectives and believe that students from different academic backgrounds bring unique insights to policy analysis. Whether you are studying economics, government, engineering, human development, or any other field, you can contribute to and benefit from APPS.
What skills will I develop as an APPS member?
Through your involvement with APPS, you will develop:
  • Analytical skills: learn to evaluate complex policy issues and data.
  • Research skills: conduct thorough policy research using various sources.
  • Communication skills: present findings clearly in written reports and presentations.
  • Teamwork: collaborate effectively with diverse team members.
  • Project management: manage timelines, deliverables, and client expectations.
  • Professional development: gain real-world consulting experience.
Will APPS help with my career goals?
Yes. APPS provides excellent preparation for careers in:
  • Public policy consulting.
  • Government and public service.
  • Government affairs.
  • Think tanks and research organizations.
  • Nonprofit management.
  • Graduate programs in public policy, law, or related fields.
  • Advocacy-related roles.
The practical experience, professional skills, and networking opportunities you gain through APPS are highly valued by employers and graduate programs.
